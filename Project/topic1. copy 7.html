Marriage

David A. Bednar

"Marriage is ordained of God. It is not an invention of man. Marriage is essential to His eternal plan. The Lord has said, 'It is not good that the man should be alone.' Adam was incomplete without Eve."

Marriage Is Essential to His Eternal Plan June 2006

Gordon B. Hinckley

"The happiest marriages I have seen are those in which there is a strong partnership between husband and wife; where there is a measure of equality; where there is an environment of respect and appreciation; where decisions are made, essentially, unanimously."

Ensign, Mar. 1997, 2

Russell M. Nelson

"Marriage is a partnership of equals, with neither exercising dominion over the other, but, rather, with each encouraging and assisting the other in whatever responsibilities and aspirations he or she might have."

Ensign, Nov. 2006, 37–38

Thomas S. Monson

"Choose a companion carefully and prayerfully; and when you are married, be fiercely loyal one to another. Priceless advice comes from a small framed plaque I once saw in the home of an uncle and aunt. It read, 'Choose your love; love your choice.'"

Ensign, May 1994, 51

Howard W. Hunter

"No other single influence in our lives compares with the influence of a righteous woman. … There are few things more powerful than the prayers of a righteous mother."

Ensign, Nov. 1994, 92

Joseph Fielding Smith

"True love does not come by chance. It is an acquired quality, like the love of God. You have to learn to love by practicing love."

Doctrines of Salvation, comp. Bruce R. McConkie, 3 vols. (1954–56), 2:333

Spencer W. Kimball

"Marriage can be more an exultant ecstasy than the human mind can conceive. This is within the reach of every couple, every person."

Faith Precedes the Miracle (1972), 142

David O. McKay

"The most important single thing that any Latter-day Saint ever does in this world is to marry the right person, in the right place, by the right authority."

Gospel Ideals (1953), 473

Elaine S. Dalton

"Marriage is the highest state of friendship. If happy, it lessens our cares by dividing them, at the same time that it doubles our pleasures by mutual participation."

Ensign, May 2000, 15

Cheryl A. Esplin

"Marriage is the ideal setting for overcoming any tendency to be selfish or self-centered. It is a union that fosters growth and selflessness."

Ensign, May 2013, 18

Summary

Marriage is ordained of God. Marriage is the relationship that God has ordained between man and women. Marriage is for those that are in love. Marriage is for those who wish to work and build upon their Love by exercising trust in each other and in jesus Christ. Our goal should be to eventually be married in the temple and to be sealed eternally to our companion. As we continue in marriage, to raise children in righteous paths. This is what can bring us one of the sweetest joys in life.It is often said that we are to be our eternal Partners better. We are to seek what's best for them and there to seek what's best for us
